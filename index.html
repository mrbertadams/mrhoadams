Biography

Biography of the Late Mr. Henry Ofoli Adams

Early Life
Henry Ofoli Adams was born on December 7, 1948, to Mr. Joseph Adama Adams of Osu and Madam Theodora Adukwei Allotey of Sempe both of blessed memory. He spent his early years in Lagos Town, now known as Accra New Town.

Education
Henry began school in 1954 at Accra New Town Primary School. After passing the Common Entrance Examination in Primary Six, he gained admission to West Africa Secondary School (WASS). However, due to bullying, likely tied to his small size, his parents withdrew him and enrolled him in Accra New Town Middle School. Two years later, he returned to WASS, where he completed his secondary and Sixth Form education.

He was a bright and confident student, representing his school in spelling competitions and taking an active role in sports.

Career
After completing his National Service at the Ghana Broadcasting Corporation (GBC), Henry was retained as a radio technician. He served in Kumasi and Konongo before returning to the GBC Head Office in Kanda, Accra.

In 1981, he moved to Nigeria, where he spent two years before returning home in 1983. Back in Ghana, he joined the Non Formal Education Division as a Ga language teacher. His passion for the language led him to write about ten Ga textbooks for basic schools, many of which are still used today. His contributions earned him a national award, presented at the Golden Tulip Hotel in Accra.

Henry was also a respected private tutor, particularly in neighborhoods like Kokomlemle, Alajo, and Accra New Town. He became widely known as ‚ÄúTeacher H O,‚Äù a name that reflected both his role and the admiration people had for him.

Interests and Personality
Henry was a man of many interests. He enjoyed reading, writing poetry, watching the news, and following football, especially his favorite team, Accra Hearts of Oak. He once participated in the popular ‚ÄúWhat Do You Know?‚Äù quiz competition and won, returning home to cheers and a festive welcome from neighborhood children.

Known for always carrying a black bag full of Ga books, he readily shared them with anyone eager to learn. He was generous with his time and knowledge.

Family and Community
Henry was a committed family man who played active roles at family events, often serving as secretary or MC. His strong tenor voice announcing ‚ÄúAgooo Awomei ke Ataamei‚Äù became his signature opening at gatherings, setting the tone for order and respect.

He was intelligent, witty, kind hearted, and fair minded. A natural mediator, he helped resolve disputes with wisdom and care. His advice was sought and valued by many.

Church Life
Henry was a devout Christian. He started in the Methodist Church, where he met and married his wife, Madam Esther Martins. Both were members of the church choir. After returning from Nigeria, he joined the Presbyterian Church in Accra New Town and served faithfully in the Men‚Äôs Fellowship, including a long stint as Secretary.

Later, he became a member of St. Cecilia Anglican Church, where he worshipped until his passing.
